History & Culture

1990's:
Foundations

1990: Established

Tom Smith, with 21 years of experience in the grocery industry, started a company making custom canvas boat covers, addressing a personal need he had as a boater.

1993

Invader and TMC pontoons were CBC's first new boat brands.

1996 – PRESENT

The iconic "Your wife called and said it was OK to buy a boat" sign was hung, followed by "your kids are texting too"

1997 – 2017

The red Glastron is hung in front of CBC and becomes a landmark off of the I-25 corridor.

1999

CBC purchased property of current location.

2000’s:

Growth & Recognition

2000 – 2008

Tom Smith was an active member of the Glastron dealer board and contributed to many of the innovations and success behind the brand before it was sold. To this day, CBC displays an availability board of current inventory offering No hassle, No haggle pricing for CBC boat buyers.

2005 – PRESENT

CBC offers first Women Making Waves class. Followed by Men In the Know in 2006.

2005 – PRESENT

CBC achieves Marine Industry Certification.

CBC expanded its product lineup and continued to focus on customer satisfaction.
